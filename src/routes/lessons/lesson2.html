LESSON 2: MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATIONS

Overview

This lesson will explore the mathematical principles behind metaballs, focusing on how they are defined and interact in a scalar field.

Key Concepts

  • Scalar Fields: Metaballs are defined by scalar fields that assign values to points in space. Their strength diminishes as the distance from the metaball's center increases:
    \( f(x, y) = \frac{r^2}{(x - x_0)^2 + (y - y_0)^2} \)

  • Combining Metaballs: Combine metaballs by summing their scalar fields:
    \( F(x, y) = \sum_{i=1}^n f_i(x, y) \)

  • Thresholding: A visible contour is formed where the scalar field exceeds a threshold value:
    \( F(x, y) \geq T \)

Applications

  • Blending Effects: Smoothly combine metaballs by adjusting their positions and the threshold value.
  • Parameter Control: Experiment with radii (\( r \)) and thresholds (\( T \)) to influence shape and size.

Practice Exercise

Calculate and visualize the scalar field for two metaballs. Adjust their positions and observe the blending behavior.
