LESSON 1: WHAT ARE METABALLS?

Metaballs are a key aspect in computer graphics which allows one to create smooth, organic, fluid-like shapes. These types of shapes are great for making dynamic effects that replicate things in the real world, such as water, lava, or simple blobs.

Metaballs are each defined as a scalar field, with its strength decreasing with its distance from the center.

The formula for a single metaball is:

\[ f(x, y) = \frac{r^2}{(x - x_0)^2 + (y - y_0)^2} \]

where \( r \) is the radius and \( (x_0, y_0) \) is the center of the metaball.
